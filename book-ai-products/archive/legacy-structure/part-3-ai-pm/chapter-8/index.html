Chapter 8

The AI PM Toolkit

Frameworks, Evals, and Experimentation

Your AI feature works in testing. It works in staging. It works in your eval suite. Then it launches, and user feedback is brutal: the responses are inconsistent, the cost is higher than expected, and your metrics show nothing you can act on. You are flying blind.

Traditional product management tools do not work for AI. You need a specialized toolkit for eval-first development, LLM-as-judge evaluation, and probabilistic experimentation. This chapter gives you that toolkit.

Objective: Equip PMs with AI-specific tools including eval-first PRDs, LLM-as-judge, and experimentation methods.

Chapter Overview

This chapter equips product managers with essential tools for AI product development. You will learn the adapted USID.O framework tailored for AI products, eval-driven development methodology using LLM-as-judge, and experimentation methods for controlled AI feature deployment. These tools form the foundation of professional AI product management today.

The key insight is that AI products require fundamentally different management approaches than traditional software. The evaluation frameworks you use must account for probabilistic outputs, the experimentation methods must handle high variance, and the metrics must balance technical performance with business outcomes. This chapter provides all of these tools in a practical, immediately applicable format.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply the USID.O framework adapted for AI products
  • Create eval-first PRDs with golden datasets
  • Implement LLM-as-judge for automated evaluation
  • Apply A/B testing, canary, and shadow deployment strategies
  • Use AI tools to accelerate PM work

Prerequisites

Readers should have a basic understanding of product management concepts and familiarity with AI/ML fundamentals. Knowledge of traditional PRD structures is helpful but not required. If you are new to PM, focus on Section 8.1 (USID.O Framework) first as it provides the conceptual foundation for everything else.
