Discovery, Prioritization, and Roadmapping

Handling Uncertainty in AI Products

Objective: Handle uncertainty in AI products through adapted research, prioritization, and data strategies.

Chapter Overview

This chapter covers how to navigate the unique challenges of AI product discovery and planning. You will learn adapted user research methods for AI, explicit kill criteria for AI feature launches, AI-enhanced roadmapping with model and data dependencies, and data strategy decisions between RAG, fine-tuning, or both. These techniques will help you make better decisions when building products where outcomes are uncertain.

The fundamental challenge in AI product development is handling uncertainty at multiple levels: uncertainty about whether AI can solve the problem, uncertainty about which approach will work best, and uncertainty about how users will respond to probabilistic outputs. This chapter provides frameworks for making decisions under these conditions while minimizing wasted effort on approaches that will not pan out.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply adapted user research methods for AI products
  • Define explicit kill criteria before launching AI features
  • Handle model and data dependencies in roadmaps
  • Choose between RAG, fine-tuning, or hybrid approaches
  • Build feedback loops for continuous AI improvement

Prerequisites

Readers should have completed Chapter 7 on the AI PM Toolkit and understand eval-driven development concepts. Familiarity with product discovery and prioritization frameworks is recommended. If you are new to discovery, focus on Section 8.1 on user research methods first.
