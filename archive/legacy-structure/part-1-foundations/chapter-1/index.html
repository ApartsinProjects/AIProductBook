Chapter 1

AI Capabilities and Limitations That Shape Products

You ship an AI feature on Monday. By Tuesday morning, users report that your model is confidently making up product names that never existed. By Wednesday, you are firefighting. By Friday, you are questioning whether AI is ready for production at all.

This scenario plays out in companies every week. Not because AI is broken, but because teams do not share a common understanding of what AI can reliably do. The solution is simpler than you think: a shared vocabulary and grounded expectations.

Objective: Ground readers in what AI can (and cannot) do reliably today; build shared vocabulary across your team.

Chapter Overview

This chapter grounds you in what AI can and cannot do reliably today. You will build shared vocabulary across your team and understand the key capabilities and limitations that shape product decisions. From reasoning models to agentic AI, from long context windows to persistent hallucinations, this chapter provides the foundation you need to make informed AI product decisions.

The chapter covers the evolution of foundation models, the emergence of agentic AI systems, key advancements, persistent limitations, and the product implications of these capabilities. By the end, you will understand when to use agents vs. simple RAG vs. fine-tuning, and how to navigate the build/buy/bake decision framework.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the evolution of foundation models from 2023 to today
  • Navigate the AI landscape: reasoning models, multimodal, open-source
  • Comprehend agentic AI capabilities and tool use
  • Apply hallucination mitigation strategies
  • Make informed build/buy/bake decisions

Prerequisites

This chapter is accessible to developers of all skill levels. Basic programming knowledge is helpful but not strictly required. No prior AI or ML experience is needed. If you are new to AI products, start here. If you have experience, this chapter will align your team around shared vocabulary and frameworks.
