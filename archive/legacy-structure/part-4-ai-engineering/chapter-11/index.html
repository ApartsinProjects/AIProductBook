Chapter 11

Core Principles of Modern AI Engineering

From PoC to Production

Your proof-of-concept works perfectly in development. It uses the latest models, has perfect accuracy in testing, and impresses everyone in the demo. Then it hits production, and latency triples, costs explode, and the model starts producing outputs you cannot debug. The PoC becomes a cautionary tale.

PoC code is experimental. Production code is engineered. The difference is not the code, it is the mindset. This chapter gives you the core principles that shift you from PoC to production.

Objective: Shift mindset from PoC to production with core engineering principles.

Chapter Overview

This chapter establishes the foundational principles for modern AI engineering. You will learn the essential skills for modern full-stack AI engineers, the three core principles (eval-driven, cost-aware, observable), and the layered architecture of the AI stack from compute to tooling. These principles will guide every technical decision you make in building production AI systems.

The transition from proof-of-concept to production AI systems requires a fundamental shift in thinking. Where PoC code can be messy and experimental, production systems must be reliable, cost-effective, and observable. This chapter provides the mental models and frameworks that enable that transition.

Learning Objectives

  • Develop the full-stack AI engineer skillset for today
  • Apply eval-driven development methodology
  • Implement cost-aware AI system design
  • Build observable AI systems with proper instrumentation
  • Understand the layers of the AI stack

Prerequisites

Readers should have completed Part III (AI PM) or have equivalent knowledge of AI product development. Familiarity with software engineering fundamentals and basic Python is expected. If you are new to AI engineering, pay special attention to Section 11.2 on the three core principles.
