Chapter 18

Real-World Case Studies

Learning from AI Product Journeys

Perplexity achieved a $14 billion valuation with a RAG-based search product. Intercom's Fin reached 81% automation with a multi-agent system. Sora, despite massive resources, struggled to find product-market fit. The difference between success and failure is not the technology, it is how you apply it.

This chapter extracts the patterns that made real AI products work and the mistakes that caused others to fail. Apply these lessons to skip the learning curve and ship faster.

Objective: Learn from real-world AI products and their journeys from vibe to production.

Chapter Overview

This chapter presents real-world case studies of AI products. You will learn from Perplexity's RAG-based search architecture, self-hosted alternatives, Intercom Fin AI's multi-agent vertical product with 81% automation, and key lessons on cost, evals, and what can go wrong. These case studies provide invaluable practical knowledge that cannot be gained from theory alone.

The most valuable lessons come from both successes and failures. We will examine products that have shipped at scale and extract the patterns that made them successful, as well as products that have struggled to find product-market fit to understand what went wrong.

Learning Objectives

  • Analyze real-world AI product architectures
  • Compare managed vs self-hosted AI solutions
  • Understand multi-agent product patterns
  • Learn from failures (Sora, Klarna, Humane)
  • Apply lessons to your own AI product journey

Prerequisites

Readers should have completed Chapter 17 on the integrated playbook. Knowledge of RAG, agents, and AI product metrics is expected. This chapter builds on the technical and strategic foundations from earlier parts.
