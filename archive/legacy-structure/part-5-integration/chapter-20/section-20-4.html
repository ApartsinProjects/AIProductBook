20.4 Your Personal Roadmap: 30/60/90-Day Plan

The concepts in this book are most powerful when applied. This section provides a structured 30/60/90-day roadmap that translates knowledge into action. Whether you're a developer transitioning into AI or a PM responsible for AI products, these timelines provide a clear path forward.

Why This Matters

Without a structured plan, learning stays theoretical. The difference between knowing about eval-driven development and actually practicing it is a concrete action plan. The timelines below give you that structure, with each phase building on the previous.

Fun Note: The 90-Day Plan That Took 90 Minutes

A PM wrote a 30-page 90-day AI readiness plan. Leadership read the first 3 pages and approved a $50K experimentation budget. The PM realized the plan was too long to read, not too short. The lesson: a plan that gets executed beats a perfect plan that stays on the shelf. Start small, learn fast.

For Developers: Building AI Product Skills

Days 1-30: Foundation Building

Primary Focus: Master One Vibe Coding Tool
  • Choose a tool: Cursor, Lovable, Bolt, or v0 - pick one and go deep
  • Complete tutorials: Do the official getting started, not just surface exploration
  • Build one project: A small complete project (todo app, simple API, basic RAG)
  • Focus on prompt engineering: Learn how to get better results through better prompts
Secondary: Start Learning
  • Begin one course: fast.ai, Hugging Face, or similar
  • Read: "Designing Machine Learning Systems" by Chip Huyen (first 3 chapters)
  • Follow AI developers on social media to stay current

Days 31-60: Practical Application

Primary Focus: Build First RAG Pipeline
  • Choose a domain: Pick something relevant to your work or interest
  • Collect data: Even a small set (10-20 documents) is enough to start
  • Build the pipeline: Embed, store, retrieve, generate
  • Create an eval: Define 10-20 test cases with expected outputs
  • Iterate: Try different chunking, retrieval, and prompting approaches
Secondary: Join Community
  • Participate in AI developer communities
  • Attend local meetups or virtual events
  • Contribute to open source or share your work

Days 61-90: Production Readiness

Primary Focus: Ship a Production AI Feature
  • Pick an existing project: Take your RAG or other project further
  • Add production elements: Observability, error handling, rate limiting
  • Deploy: Ship to a real environment (not just local)
  • Monitor: Track cost, latency, quality metrics
  • Iterate: Use production data to improve
Secondary: Mentor Others
  • Share what you've learned with your team
  • Document your journey (blog, internal docs)
  • Help others get started

For Product Managers: Leading AI Products

Days 1-30: Foundation Building

Primary Focus: Define Eval Criteria for Your Product
  • Identify quality dimensions: What does "good" look like for your product?
  • Create test cases: Start with 10 examples of good and bad outputs
  • Define metrics: How will you measure each quality dimension?
  • Get buy-in: Align with engineering on what's measurable
Secondary: Learn the Technology
  • Complete a basic LLM course (doesn't need to be deep)
  • Use ChatGPT/Claude actively to understand capabilities
  • Talk to your AI engineers - ask them to explain their work

Days 31-60: Practical Application

Primary Focus: Run Your First A/B Test
  • Design the test: What's the hypothesis? What's the control?
  • Define success: What metrics matter? What's the minimum detectable effect?
  • Set up tracking: Ensure you can measure the right things
  • Run the test: Ship to a small segment, monitor results
  • Analyze: What did you learn? What do you do next?
Secondary: Understand Cost
  • Calculate cost per user session for your AI features
  • Model different usage scenarios
  • Discuss cost optimization with engineering

Days 61-90: Organizational Impact

Primary Focus: Establish Cost Monitoring
  • Build a dashboard: Track AI costs by feature, user, time
  • Set alerts: What triggers cost concerns?
  • Create processes: How do you respond to cost spikes?
  • Report regularly: Make cost visible to stakeholders
Secondary: Improve Processes
  • Introduce eval reviews to your team rhythm
  • Propose a framework for AI feature specs
  • Start a community of practice for AI products

Adjusting for Your Context

These roadmaps are starting points. Adjust based on your situation:

If You're Already Working on AI Products
  • Skip foundational steps and focus on gaps
  • Identify your weakest area (cost, eval, production) and prioritize
  • Share your experience to help others
If You're in a Large Organization
  • Expect more process and review cycles
  • Focus on building cross-functional relationships
  • Look for quick wins to demonstrate value
If You're at a Startup
  • Move faster - compress timelines
  • Focus on what's critical, not what's ideal
  • Embrace the chaos - you'll learn faster
Key Takeaways
  • 30 days: Focus on foundation - tools for developers, eval for PMs
  • 60 days: Focus on application - build something, test something
  • 90 days: Focus on production and organization
  • Adjust timelines to your context and experience level
  • The most important step is the first one - start today
